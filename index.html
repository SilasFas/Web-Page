Android logo History

You probably know that the Android operating system maintained by Google is one of the most used for mobile devices worldwide. But maybe you don't know that its friendly mascot has a name and a very curious story. Well, follow this article to learn a lot about this little robot.

The first version

The first attempt to come up with a mascot came in 2007 and came from a developer named Dan Morrill . He says he opened Inkscape (free software for vectorizing images) and created his own version of the robot. The goal was just to personify the system only for his team, there was no request from the company to create a mascot.

This terrible first version was even named after its creator: it would be the Dandroids.

A new mascot comes to the scene

The idea of having a mascot matured and the mission was passed on to a professional in the area. Russian illustrator Irina Blok , also a Google employee, was tasked with representing the little robot in a more pleasant way.

Irina's main idea was to represent everything graphically with few strokes and in a flatter way. The design should also bring quick identification with the person looking at it. Then came the Bugdroid, the new mascot of Android.

The main inspiration for the features of the new Bugdroid came from those little figures that illustrate bathroom doors to indicate the gender of each door. People say that the artist was working at her desk in the Google office and looked at the restrooms and the identification was immediate: simple, straightforward, clear.

Want to learn more?

Another curious thing about Android is that each version has always been named after a candy, in alphabetical order from version 1.5 to 9.0.

Unfortunately, Android Q did not exist, as Google decided to put an end to this fun practice and started using numbers, which gave rise to Android 10 .

Access the Android History website here to learn about the sequence of "sweetened" versions and what each one brought to the Android system.
